  • The second section of the document discusses the infrequent and exception use cases.

Terminology & Requirements

Roles

  1. Customer - End user for the BB Player. Typically a child with little or no computer experience.
  2. Retailer - Store owner who has signed up to sell BB, eTickets and BB Player accessories. Typically will have little or no computer experience. The store will have limited amount of space available for BB Depot Stations.

BB Player

  1. BB Player - hardware bought by customer for playing content.
  2. BB Card - removable memory card/module that contains the customers eTickets and content and plugs into the BB Player.
  3. BB Safety Box or BB Game Vault - A customer gets rights to play content through the purchase of an eTicket. The content that he owns may be backed up to this Safety Box on the Server from where he may retrieve it any time at no cost.
  4. Audio Alert - audible beep used to indicate data entry and completion of tasks to the customer
  5. Authorized Retailer - Retailers with BB Depots who sell BB Players, accessories, eTickets and accept units for repair.

BB Depot 

  1. BB Depot - computer used by the customer for conducting BB transactions
  2. BB Card Reader - a slot on the BB Depot into which a BB Card can be inserted by the customer
  3. Game Card - A small plastic or paper card with a magnetic stripe (similar to a credit card) which is used to "pay" for games.
  4. Game Card Reader - a magnetic stripe reader
  5. Depot Console - SVGA or LCD screen with touch screen capability
  6. BB Depot USB ports - at least 2 ports: one for the BB Card Reader and one for the Game Card Reader
  7. Audio Alert - audible beep used to indicate data entry and completion of tasks to the customer

Server

  1. Servers - refers to the transactional systems at the Ops Center. The Servers must always authenticate themselves to the BB Depot or BB Player if involved in any transaction.
  2. RMS - Remote Management Servers
  3. CDS - Content Distribution Servers

Section 1 : Mainstream Use Cases

Content Purchase

Description: Customer selects and "pays" for a piece of content, and upon successful completion, owns the content for playing on his BB Player.

Rationale

  1. eTicket i.e. content license is individualized for the BB Player. Possession of the content alone does not indicate ownership. 

Requirements

  1. Customer must be able to identify the BB Card that belongs to him via some visible mark without plugging in the card. In addition customer may be permitted to enter some personalization information that is stored in the BB Card and viewable by the BB Player or BB Depot.
  2. Customer has previously selected the content that he wishes to purchase and has a Game Card of the appropriate value to purchase that content.

Assumptions & conditions

  1. Customer has purchased a BB Player and he has an individualized BB Card for his BB Player. Customer is responsible for ensuring that he does not bring in the BB Card belonging to some other BB Player.
  2. The BB Card has a BBID that uniquely identifies (does not authenticate) the BB Player for which the eTicket will be issued.
  3. Customer does not have to bring in his BB Player.

Sequence of events/actions

Customer Retailer
  1. Pays the retailer for a Game Card of sufficient value to purchase the desired content
  
 
  1. Issues a Game Card to the customer
  1. Plugs BB Card into the BB Depot's BB Card Reader
  2. Selects the content to be purchased
  3. If space is available, the console prompts the user to swipe their Game Card. Otherwise, the user is taken to the "Content Backup to Safety Box" section.
  4. After the content (data and eTicket) is successfully installed on the BB Card, the customer removes their BB Card and the transaction is complete.
 

Error/Failure Conditions

  1. Servers and BB Depot fail to mutually authenticate each other.
  2. BBID is not recognized by the Server as identifying an official BB Player.
  3. ContentID is no longer available for purchase.
  4. No response is received from the Server.
  5. Customer is not able to provide Game Cards with sufficient value for purchase of the content.

Content Backup to Safety Box

Description: Customer backs up content to Safety Box .

Rationale

  1. Customer will be prompted only when he wants to download a piece of content (either a new purchase or a restoration from their Safety Box) and the BB Card does not have enough free space for downloading that content.

Requirements

  1. Memory capacity and management must be easily understandable by the customer, who may have very little computer knowledge. The customer base includes small children who have limited comprehension of large numbers.

Assumptions & conditions

  1. The BB Card has enough memory to hold the new content, if some of the old ones are backed up to the Safety Box.
  2. Retailer does not have to authenticate the depot the the Server.

Sequence of events/actions

Customer Retailer
  1. Customer requests additional content be added to their BB Card; either through a new purchase or a restoration from their Safety Box.
  2. Depot detects BB Card does not have enough free space, and list all titles of content currently stored on the BB Card. Asks customer to select a title to be backed up to Safety Box.
  3. Customer selects content to be backed up.
  4. Customer repeats the previous step. As soon as enough memory space has been freed, content download resumes automatically.
 

Error/Failure Conditions

  1. BB Depot is not able to communicate with the BB Card.
  2. New content is too big to fit in the current BB Card.
  3. No response is received from the BB Depot.

Content Restore from Safety Box

Description: Customer downloads content (which he already has an eTicket for) onto his BB Card.

Rationale

  1. Customer may own a large number of eTickets, but have space for a limited number of pieces of content on his BB Card. So, he may constantly be backing up and restoring content onto the BB Card from his virtual Safety Box.

Requirements

  1. It must be possible to automatically download promotional content as part of regular content downloads if space is available. Customer must not have to manage space used by promotional content.

Assumptions & conditions

  1. The BB Card identifies (does not authenticate) the BB Player for which the eTicket has been purchased. The actual BB Player does not need to be present.
  2. The BB Player owns at least one eTicket which identifies the content.

Sequence of events/actions

Customer Retailer
  1. Inserts his BB Card into the BB Card Reader
  2. The Depot Console displays a list of the content currently installed on the BB Card, and also the content available in the Safety Box.
  3. Using the touch screen on the Depot Console, the customer selects the content to be restored from the Safety Box.
  4. If space is available, the content is restored. Otherwise, the user is taken to the "Content Backup to Safety Box" section.
  5. Waits for the display to indicate completion of the download process
  6. This process may be repeated as many times as necessary. The customer simply removes their BB Card when done.
  

Error/Failure Conditions

  1. BB Card is not initialized or BBID is not recognized by the Server as identifying an official BB Player.

Section 2 : Infrequent & Exception Use Cases

BB Card Repair

Description: Customer wants to repair his BB Card.. 

Rationale

  1. Customer receives visual and/or audible indication that his BB Card needs to be repaired.

Assumptions & conditions

  1. Content of BB Card might be corrupted or not initialized.
  2. Customer brings in the BB Player along with the BB Card to the Store. 

Sequence of events/actions

Customer Retailer
  1. Takes BB Player and the BB Card to the Store.
  2. Plugs in the BB Card into the BB Depot
  
 
  1. Depot recognizes BB Card as in need of repair and proceeds to do that.
  2. If the repair is successful (i.e., no hardware failure), asks the Customer to remove BB Card from the port and plug it into the BB Player. Otherwise, tells customer to buy a new card (see BB Card Purchase below).
  1. Plugs BB Card into the BB Player to individualize card which is done automatically.
  2. Customer may now plug in the new BB Card into the BB Depot to retrieve content from their Safety Box.
  

Error/Failure Conditions

  1. BB Card cannot be repaired because of hardware failure.
  2. BB Card cannot be individualized because the BB Player is not recognized as an official unit.
  3. BB Card cannot be individualized because the BB Player does not respond.

BB Card Purchase

Description: Customer purchases another BB Card.

Rationale

  1. Customer might need to purchase a new BB Card to replace a lost/damaged card.
  2. Customer might want to purchase additional BB Cards so that he can have more content available to him without returning to the BB Depot to swap content.

Assumptions & conditions

  1. Customer has in his possession a purchased BB Player which he brings to the store. 
  2. All new BB Cards have been initialized at the factory.

Sequence of events/actions

Customer Retailer
  1. Asks for a new BB Card.
  2. Hands over money to the Retailer.
  
 
  1. Accepts money from customer and hands over new BB Card
  1. Plugs in a new BB Card to BB Player for individualization.
  2. Customer may now plug in the new BB Card into the BB Depot to retrieve content from their Safety Box.
  

Error/Failure Conditions

  1. Servers and BB Depot fail to mutually authenticate each other.
  2. BB Card cannot be individualized because the BB Player does not respond.
  3. BB Player is not able to communicate with the new BB Card.
  4. No response is received from the BB Depot or the Server.

BB Player Repair

Description: Customer wishes to get his BB Player repaired.

Rationale

  1. A very small percentage of BB Players may malfunction. Customer must have a way of getting these repaired at authorized Retailers.

Requirements

  1. A Regional Operations Center must offer its repair services to the Retailer.

Assumptions & conditions

  1. Customer has proof of purchase.
  2. Customer is able to describe what is wrong with the system.
  3. Not an online or secure operation and the BB Depot is not involved in the transaction.

Sequence of events/actions - BB Player drop-off

Customer Retailer
  1. Customer brings in BB Player along with any accessories which may be malfunctioning.
  2. Customer describes problem to the Retailer.
  
  1. Retailer verifies the problem if possible, to ensure that is is not a pilot error.
  2. Retailer fills out a 'Repair Sheet' form and describes the problem.
  3. Retailer asks the customer for  receipt/proof-of-purchase that the customer provides.
  4. If the BB Player is not in the warranty period, Retailer is asked to charge the customer. 
  5. Retailer calls in and receives an RMA number,  the address where to send the Player and the expected number of days that it will take to repair the unit. 
  6. Retailer gives customer a receipt with the RMA number.
  7. Retailer packs BB Player and accessories and sends it along with the RMA number to the Regional Operations Center.

Sequence of events/actions - BB Player pick up

Customer Retailer
  1. Customer gives Retailer receipt with RMA number printed on it.
  1. Retailer verifies that he has received the appropriate box from the Regional Operational Center.
  2. Retailer is advised on the status of the repair and any outstanding charges.
  3. Status and charges vary depending upon whether the BB Player and/or accessories
    • were within the warranty period
    • whether it was repairable
    • whether a replacement unit was shipped if it was not repairable
  4. Retailer completes any monetary transaction and gives repaired/replacement BB Player and accessories to the customer.

BB Player Return

Description: Customer returns a recently purchased BB Player.

Rationale

  1. A small percentage of returns are part of normal customer behavior.

 Assumptions & conditions

  1. The BB Player Return is purely between the customer and the retailer. The retailer is permitted to follow any policy he prefers.

Requirements

  1. Not an online or secure operation and the BB Depot is not involved in the transaction.

Sequence of events/actions

Customer Retailer
  1. Hands over BB Player to Retailer and asks to return BB
  
 
  1. Depending upon the condition of the BB Player, packaging, BB Card, the Retailer follows his store policy wrt to accepting returns.
  2. It should be noted that the customer gives up ownership of any additional eTickets that he may have purchased along with the BB Player, if the retailer accepts the return.
  3. The Retailer is free to resell any returned BB Players.

Error/Failure Conditions

  1. Retailer can verify that the BB Player is functional by hooking it up to a TV and playing content. 

Content Refund

Description: Customer wishes to return a piece of content that he has purchased.

Rationale

  1. All sales are final. We do not allow for return of individual eTickets.
