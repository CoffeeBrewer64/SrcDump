    BB Player Purchase

    Description: Customer purchases BB player.

    Rationale

    1. The customer experience must be smooth and we must adhere to existing purchase models.
    2. It must be possible to sell BB Players at stores without any BB Depots.
    3. A customer must be able to purchase the BB Player as a gift without having to learn our eTicket model. 

    Requirements

    1. BB Player is shipped from the factory with an individualized BB Card in one package. This BB Card contains any Free Content along with the eTicket to play it. 
    2. It must be possible to create Value Packs for customers where we bundle in multiple content pieces with the associated eTickets to play them. This must not require the customer to have to spend time at the store to incur any additional cost in terms of purchase or download steps. 
    3. Customer must be able to easily remove the BB Card which comes with the BB Player to purchase additional content at the retailer's store without opening the entire package.
    4. Not an online or secure operation and the BB Depot is not involved in the transaction.

    Assumptions & conditions

    1. The Free Content does not have any cash value associated with it and may not be returned for any cash refund.
    2. The Free Content may be changed from time to time at the factory, but cannot be changed once the BB Player is shipped from the factory.

      BB Player Return

      Description: Customer returns a recently purchased BB Player.

      Rationale

      1. A small percentage of returns are part of normal customer behavior.

       Assumptions & conditions

      1. The BB Player Return is purely between the customer and the retailer. The retailer is permitted to follow any policy he prefers.

      Requirements

      1. Not an online or secure operation and the BB Depot is not involved in the transaction.

      Customer Retailer

      Description: Customer wishes to return an eTicket that he has purchased.

      Rationale

      1. All sales are final. We do not allow for return of individual eTickets. Since the customer selects the actual content himself before the purchase is made, there is no chance of the retailer making a mistaken sale.

          Content Backup to Safety Box

          Description: Customer backs up content to Safety Box .

          Rationale

          1. Customer will be prompted only when he wants to download a piece of content and the BB Card does not have enough free space for downloading that content.

          Requirements

          1. Memory capacity and management must be easily understandable by the customer and the retailer, both of who may have very little computer knowledge. The customer base includes small children who have limited comprehension capability of large numbers.

          Assumptions & conditions

          1. Assumptions & conditions for Content Download have been met.
          2. The BB Card has enough memory to hold the new content, if some of the old ones are backed up to the Safety Box.
          3. Retailer does not have to authenticate the depot the the Server.

          Sequence of events/actions

          Customer Retailer
          1. Carries out Content Download procedure.
          2. Download Station detects BB Card does not have enough free space, and list all titles of contents currently stored on the BB Card. Ask customer to select the title to be backed up to Safety Box on the Server.
          3. Using the BB controller on the Download Station, customer selects content to be backed up.
          4. Customer repeats the previous step. As soon as enough memory space has been freed, Download Station resumes automatically to download the selected content.
          5. Removes BB Card.
          		  

          Error/Failure Conditions

          1. Download Station is not able to communicate with the BB Card.
          2. New content is too big to fit in the current BB Card - customer will be prompted for a memory upgrade.
          3. No response is received from the BB Depot.
