User model outlines the customer or retailer experience as they go about their roles. This document is split into two parts:

  • The first part of the document lists out the Mainstream User Model cases.
  • The second part of the document discusses the exception or not so common cases.

Mainstream User Model cases

Customer

  1. BB Player purchase
  2. BB Player return
  3. eTicket Purchase
  4. eTicket Refund
  5. Content Download
  6. Purchase expansion memory for BB Card

BB Player Purchase

Description: Customer purchases BB player, and gets any free content associated with the purchase of the BB.

Rationale

  1. Ensure that only officially manufactured BB units are sold through BB Depots and can subsequently purchase content (i.e. eTickets).
  2. Promotional/free content eTicket will be issued at the time of the BB Player purchase. This content may change from time to time based on marketing promotions and therefore cannot be fixed at the time of manufacture.

 Assumptions & conditions

  1. Customer may not return free content for refunds or cash.
  2. Operator/Retailer has authenticated depot to the server using depotID.

Sequence of events/actions

  1. Customer - selects or hands over BB Player to Retailer and asks to purchase BB & get Free Content
  2. Retailer - selects BB Purchase option in Retail Application & is prompted to connect the BB Player
  3. Retailer - connect special USB cable to new BB player, through a "hole" in the box.
  4. Retailer - starts the purchase process
  5. Retailer - when the display shows completion of purchase process, retailer unplugs the cable and hand the BB player to customer. Customer is directed to go to the download station to get his free game.
  6. Customer proceeds to download station and goes through with "Content Download"

BB Player Return

Description: Customer returns a recently purchased BB Player.

Rationale

  1. Provides the retailer with an option to return a recently purchased but unused BB Players back into the stock in case of any erroneous purchases.

 Assumptions & conditions

  1. After a BB Player has been purchased, a customer has up to 30 minutes during which it may be returned for a refund. This forces the customer to learn our process right away by following through with the content download soon after purchase and limits inventory accounting issues if returns were not subject to a time limit.
  2. Returns will also not be permitted if the eTicket associated with the free content is downloaded from the servers or any other eTickets associated with other content is downloaded to the BB.
  3. Operator/Retailer has authenticated depot to the server using depotID.

Sequence of events/actions

  1. Customer - hands over BB Player to Retailer and asks to return BB
  2. Retailer - selects BB Player Return option in Retail Application & is prompted to connect the BB Player
  3. Retailer - connect special USB cable to new BB player, through a "hole" in the box.
  4. Retailer - starts the return process
  5. Retailer - if return process is successful, puts BB Player back on display and refunds customer the amount of purchase. If the return process is unsuccessful, disconnects and returns BB Player back to customer.

eTicket Purchase

Description: Customer wants to purchase additional content.

Rationale

  1. eTicket i.e. content license is individualized for the BB Player. Possession of content does not indicate ownership. 

Assumptions & conditions

  1. Customer has purchased a BB Player and he has a formatted BB Card.
  2. Customer needs only the BB Card - since this contains his BB Player's Digital Certificate (DC). The DC has the public key of the BB Player and a unique BBID.
  3. The BB Card identifies (does not authenticate) the BB Player for which the eTicket will be issued.
  4. Operator/Retailer has authenticated depot to the server using depotID.

Sequence of events/actions

  1. Customer - selects a content/game title from a brochure or other material for which he wants to get the eTicket
  2. Customer - hands over his BB player's BB Card to the Retailer along with the money & indicates the content
  3. Retailer - selects Purchase eTicket option from the Retail Application
  4. Retailer - upon being prompted, plugs in the BB Card into the BB Depot
  5. Retailer - upon successful purchase returns the BB Card to the customer & asks the customer to go to the download station to download his content.
  6. Customer - walks over to the download station, inserts the BB Card.
  7. Customer - reviews display which shows all the content for which he has eTickets, associated with his BB Player as identified by the BB Card.
  8. Customer - selects the content he wants to download, confirms the selection, and removes the BB Card when the transaction is complete.

eTicket Refund

Description: Permit the customer to get his money back for an unused eTicket which was purchased by Retailer error.

Rationale

  1. The Retailer  may have made a mistake during the eTicket Purchase process or the customer may have changed his mind about the purchase.

Assumptions & conditions

  1. A refund will not be permitted if the eTicket is downloaded from the server into the BB Card.
  2. The customer also has only 30 minutes after the purchase of an eTicket to get a refund. This is to limit the exposure we have where another customer#2 with a BB Card that contains the BB identification of customer#1's BB Player asks for a refund of a recently purchased eTicket.
  3. The BB Player identified by the BB Card has recently purchased an eTicket. 
  4. The server always has the complete information about the BB Player & eTickets that are owned by it.
  5. The BB Card identifies (does not authenticate) the BB Player for which the eTicket has been.
  6. Retailer has authenticated depot to the server using depotID.

Sequence of events/actions

  1. Customer - indicates that he wants an eTicket refund, hands over the BB Card and identifies to the Retailer the content.
  2. Retailer -  selects the eTicket Refund option from the Retail Application and selects the eTicket in question
  3. Retailer - receives confirmation from the server that eTicket refund may be issued since the conditions for refund have been met
  4. Retailer - confirms eTicket refund and amount of purchase
  5. Retailer - waits for transaction to complete and hands over the BB Card and the money to the customer

Content Download

Description: Customer's BB Player owns eTickets and he wants to download the associated content into the BB Card. 

Rationale

  1. Customer may own a large number of eTickets, but have space for a limited number of content on his BB Card. So, he may constantly be deleting and reloading content into the BB Card.
  2. Content download is a separate action from eTicket Purchase, since this may take a slightly longer period of time. 

Assumptions & conditions

  1. The BB Card identifies (does not authenticate) the BB Player for which the eTicket has been. The actual BB Player does not need to be present.
  2. The BB Player owns at least one eTicket which identifies the content. 
  3. Retailer does not have to authenticate the depot the the server.

Sequence of events/actions

  1. Customer - inserts his BB Card into the download station
  2. Customer - reviews all owned eTickets
  3. Customer - using  the BB controller on the download station, customer selects content to be downloaded.
  4. Customer - waits for the display to indicate completion of the download process
  5. Customer - repeats this process for all content that he is interested in that he owns
  6. Customer - removes BB Card.

Purchase expansion memory for BB Card

Description: Enable customer to purchase extra memory for his BB Card

Assumptions & conditions

  1. Not an online or secure operation.
  2. Shop owner has already paid for the merchandises when they are delivered from the warehouse.

Sequence of events/actions

  1. Customer - pays Retailer for extra memory
  2. Customer - plugs in extra memory into BB Card 

Other User Model cases

Customer use cases - infrequent

  1. Repair BB Card
  2. Purchase BB Card
  3. BB Card used space management
  4. BB Player return without going through BB Player Purchase process.
  5. BB Player return after eTicket download or after time limit has passed

Customer use cases - exceptions

  1. Unable to contact server during the content download which follows eTicket Purchase
  2. Unable to contact server when customer wants to execute BB Player Return
  3. Unable to contact server when customer wants to execute eTicket Refund

Repair BB Card

Description: Customer has a BB Card that needs to be repaired. 

Rationale

  1. Customer receives indication that his BB Card needs to be repaired.

Assumptions & conditions

  1. BB has been activated.
  2. Customer brings in the BB Player along with the BB Card to the Retailer. 
  3. Retailer has to authenticate the depot the the server using the depotID.

Sequence of events/actions

  1. Customer - takes BB and the BB Card to the Retailer.
  2. Customer - hands over BB Player & BB Card to the Retailer.
  3. Retailer - plugs customer's BB Card into console recovery port and selects Card Repair
  4. Retailer - once the repair is complete, removes BB Card from the console recover port and plugs it into the customer's BB Player
  5. Retailer - plugs owner's BB into console activation port turns on BB and selects Card Customization
  6. Retailer - confirms completion of customization process and hands back BB Player and BB Card to the customer.
  7. Retailer - directs customer to the download station for him to load any content that he owns.
