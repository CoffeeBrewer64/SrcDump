Section 1 : Manufacturer Use Cases

Roles

  1. Chip Manufacturing Manager - Person at the chip manufacturer responsible for sending the list of digital certificates that were manufactured by the foundry.
  2. BB Player Manufacturing Manager - Person at the board manufacturer responsible for sending the list of BBIDs of chips that are incorporated into manufactured BB Players.

Use case: BB Chip Registration 

Description: Chip Manufacturing Manager sends list of BB Digital Certificates (which contains BBID & BB Public Key) to us.

Rationale
  1. The private key that is burnt into the BB chip is key to all security and is a closely guarded secret which never leaves the factory other than inside the chip.
  2. We receive the list of all chips that have been successfully manufactured. 

Requirements

  1. BB Chip Manufacture Server has received from us the Key for Browser License, the Secure Kernel Hash and the Root CA Digital Certificate.
  2. BB Chip Manufacture Server has the ability to generate good public/private key pairs, BBID and the associated Digital Certificates signed by a Manufacturing License issued by us.

Assumptions & conditions
  1. All communication is encrypted, secure & mutually authenticated.
  2. Chip manufacturer has ensured that the BBID and public key incorporated into the DC match. 

Sequence of events/actions
Chip Manufacturer Manager Operations Center
  1. Generates BBID, public/private key pairs and BB DCs.
  2. Determines which BBIDs are successfully manufactured.
  3. Securely sends to the BB Key Manager the BB DCs corresponding to these BBIDs.
  
  1. Loads BB Digital Certificates of manufactured chips into BB Key Manager.

Use case: BB Player Registration

Description: BB Player Manufacturing Manger submits new BB Player to the Operations Center.

Rationale
  1. Only officially manufactured BB Players may be purchased through our system. eTickets will only be issued to purchased BB Players. This limits the risk of a gray market for BB Players.

Requirements
  1. Manufacturer is able to carry out system level tests including reading the BBID from the manufactured BB Player.

Assumptions & conditions
  1. All communication is encrypted, secure & mutually authenticated.

Sequence of events/actions
BB Player Manufacturer Operations Center
  1. As each batch is manufactured and shipped from the factory, the information is uploaded into the Operations Center.
  
1. Loads list of BBID of manufactured BB Players into BB Key Manager.

Section 2 : Operations Center Use Cases

Roles

  1. Content Manager -  Person responsible for the Content Management System.
  2. Marketing Manager - Person responsible for determining timing and regional distribution of content and operates the Content Availability Server.

Use case: Publish Content

Description: Content Manager publishes content for general distribution.

Rationale
  1. Publishing control should be controlled by the Content Manager and not the studio.

Requirements
  1. Content has been released by the Studio and has been validated by the Content Management Servers.

Assumptions & conditions

  1. It also has to be  suitably encrypted for distribution to the BB Depots. Associated metadata has to be available for content browsing and eTicket purchase.
  2. All communication is encrypted, secure & mutually authenticated.

Sequence of events/actions
Content Manager
  1. Selects Content for publishing.
  2. Unique customer/retailer visible 5 digit ContentID is generated.
  3. Make encrypted content available to the Content Distribution Server (CDS).
  4. Make metadata (including encryption key) available to the Content Availability Server (CAS) and eTicket Server.

Use case: Distribute Content

Description: Marketing Manager sets price and makes content available on a regional basis.

Rationale

  1. Once content is published, it's availability has to be timed with suitable marketing which may happen on a regional basis. 

Requirements
  1. Content has been published by the Content Manager and released to the CDS & CAS.

Assumptions & conditions
  1. CDS has received encrypted content.
  2. CAS has received content  metadata.

Sequence of events/actions
Marketing Manager
  1. Sets price and decides on the date that content is available for purchase and download on a per region basis.

Section 3 : Studio/Content Developer Use Cases

Roles

  1. Studio Manager -  Person responsible for the Release Kit Content Release Manager.

Use case: Studio Releases a Title/Content

Description: Studio Manager releases content to the Content Management Subsystem (CMS) for further publishing.

Requirements

  1. Studio is responsible for QA of content.

Assumptions & conditions

  1. The Studio has officially signed up and has a DevKit installed in their premises.
  2. Studio is able to securely communicate with the Content Management Subsystem - encryption and data integrity are enforced. 
  3. Content Release Manager has authenticated itself to the Operations Center for online operations.

Sequence of events/actions

Studio Ops Center
  1. Studio develops content.
  2. Studio Manager uses Release Kit to package the content. The package includes the content, a hash of the content for validity check, and possibly archive all files into one file. All related metadata information (title name, description, required memory size, size of saved game, image files for ads, etc) will be included.
  3. The package will be encrypted by the public key in the certificate that had been bundled in the DevKit. 
  4. Studio delivers the release package to Operation Center. It can be done on-line (via email, http post, etc), or off-line (CD-R, tapes, etc)
 
 
  1. Loads the content and the metadata into the Content Management Subsystem.
  2. Starts the validation process.
